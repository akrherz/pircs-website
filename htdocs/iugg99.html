Advances in Regional Climate Modeling

Limited-area atmospheric numerical models have recently gained widespread use as tools for understanding regional climates. This technique is also being explored as a method for evaluating regional climate change and its impacts on human activities. This session will summarize recent progress and outstanding issues in the use of regional climate models. For this purpose "climate" simulations are defined as having duration of at least 30 days (i.e., beyond the range of deterministic weather prediction). Contributions are also invited on complementary modeling techniques, such as global variable-resolution models, and methods for processing and interpreting regional climate model results.

The regional climate modeling session is a part of a five-day (19-23 July) program on model intercomparisons, which will also include AMIP and other organized climate model intercomparison projects. The regional modeling session is scheduled for Thursday and Friday, 22-23 July 1999. (Note new date!)

The quantity and quality of abstracts received point to an excellent session. The program for the model intercomparison session (including the regional modeling session) is available on the PCMDI web site at http://www-pcmdi.llnl.gov/amip/IUGG98_MC01/prelim.html. Information on the IUGG XXII General Assembly can be found at the web site for IUGG.

Presenters at the IUGG session are encouraged to submit followup papers to a JGR special issue on regional climate modeling, which will have a manuscript due date of 1 December 1999. Contributions to the JGR issue also are welcome from those who are unable to attend the IUGG session.
