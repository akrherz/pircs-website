A session to be held at the
XXII General Assembly of the International Union of Geodesy and Geophysics
18-30 July 1999, Birmingham, U.K.



Advances in Regional Climate Modeling

Limited-area atmospheric numerical models have recently gained widespread use as tools for understanding regional climates. This technique is also being explored as a method for evaluating regional climate change and its impacts on human activities. This session will summarize recent progress and outstanding issues in the use of regional climate models. For this purpose "climate" simulations are defined as having duration of at least 30 days (i.e., beyond the range of deterministic weather prediction). Contributions are also invited on complementary modeling techniques, such as global variable-resolution models, and methods for processing and interpreting regional climate model results.

The regional climate modeling session is a part of a four-day program on model intercomparisons, which will also include AMIP and other organized climate model intercomparison projects. Presently this model comparison program is scheduled for 19-22 July, during the first week of the IUGG assembly. The regional modeling session is tentatively scheduled for Wednesday, 21 July 1999. More information on the four-day model intercomparison series is available at the PCMDI web site.

The abstract deadline for IUGG99 has been extended to 19 January 1999. Instructions for abstract submission and information on the IUGG XXII General Assembly can be found at the web site for IUGG99.

Presenters at the IUGG99 session are encouraged to submit followup papers to a JGR special issue on regional climate modeling, which will have a manuscript due date in the fall of 1999. Contributions to the JGR issue also are welcome from those who are unable to attend the IUGG session.

Abstract Submittal

Abstracts can be submitted by online web form, email, or postal mail. All abstracts must be submitted directly to the IUGG. It will be helpful to send a copy to the co-convenors, but this does not replace the requirement for submission to the IUGG.

1. Online submission can be done through the web form at the main IUGG web site: http://www.bham.ac.uk/IUGG99/abform.htm

Under the heading "Association and Symposia" click IAMAS and then in the drop-down window choose MC01 - Improvements and intercomparisons of climate system models.

2. Email your abstract to the IUGG following the instructions given on their web page: http://www.bham.ac.uk/IUGG99/abstract.htm

Note that the abstract must be sent as plain ASCII text, not an attached file. If you prepare your abstract using word processing software, copy and paste the material into your email program so that it is sent as plain text.

In the supplementary information following your abstract, for item 1 (session number) use MC01 and for item 2 (session name) use "Improvements and intercomparisons of climate system models."

3. Postal mail instructions are essentially the same as for email submission given above. For details see the IUGG web page at: http://www.bham.ac.uk/IUGG99/abstract.htm
