Interfacing Mesoscale Climate Models with Impacts Assessment

A Workshop

Agenda for Thursday
TimeLeaderTopic
7:45 a.m. Bus leaves Broker Inn for Mesa Lab
8:00 a.m. Continental breakfast at NCAR and informal introductions
8:30 a.m. Vary Welcome to Workshop and General Introduction
8:35 a.m. Blackmon Welcome to NCAR
8:40 a.m. Wigley Mesoscale-model climate simulations: Advantages & Disadvantages for Impacts Assessment
9:00 a.m. Mearns Review of Impacts Assessments using Mesoscale Climate Simulations
9:20 a.m. Kuo, Machenhauer, Pielke Panel Presentation: 15 min. presentation by each + 15 min questions. What is the intent of a mesoscale model? What is it good for? What is it not good for? What are opportunities for climate modeling? How does mesoscale-model climate simulation differ from global simulation? How does it differ from several-day simulation? What lessons can we apply from past mesoscale-modeling experience?
10:15a.m. Hakkarinen & ISU Specific mission of the meeting. Overview of discussion categories and goals for each one. Breakout-groups' members listed.
10:30 a.m. Discussion: Mode One questions [coffee served] Goal: A prioritized list of mesoscale model output fields to archive for impacts studies, including types of diagnostic (but not simulation) codes that may need to be embedded in the model. List should include frequency of output, whether output is instantaneous or cumulative. Estimates of systematic and random errors. Error tolerance for impact studies. Discussion should include and report on questions raised in preparatory white paper and additional questions we might generate.
12:00 p.m. -- lunch --
1:00 p.m. Group reports to full workshop
2:00 p.m. Giorgi How have mesoscale models for climate evolved? How are new processes embedded in a model? What are typical problems encountered in the process? What are typical readjustments made to accommodate a new process? What has driven past choices for new processes to internalize? What is gained, what is lost? (Just cpu time?)
2:30 p.m. Discussion: Mode Two questions [coffee served] Goal: A prioritized list of candidate processes for internalizing into mesoscale climate models. List should include where the processes link to the model (i.e., input to the process subroutine) and what they should return, either as model output or as feedbacks to other model processes. List should be tailored to mesoscale simulation.
4:00 p.m. Group reports to full workshop
5:00 p.m. Adjourn for the day; informal reception follows
6:00 p.m. Dinner at Mesa Lab
8:30 p.m. Bus leaves Mesa Lab for Broker Inn

TimeLeaderTopic

9:00 a.m. ISU Mesoscale simulations in support of IPCC Third Assessment Report
9:30 a.m. Discussion: Three-year runs [coffee served] Goal: Tailoring earlier recommendations for three-year simulations

List should include what types of special observations or observation campaigns might be needed for validation, potential time line for developing these models, and potential for eventually embedding these models in mesoscale climate models
