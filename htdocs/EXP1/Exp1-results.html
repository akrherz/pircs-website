TIME SERIES

Time series of surface fluxes vs. FIFE obs. To ease viewing of overall behavior, all curves have been subjected to a 5-day running average.

Two curves are shown for the FIFE output. For each time step in their data, Betts and Ball give the standard deviation of the observed values as well as their average. The FIFE curves are the average value +/- one standard deviation, to give a measure of how well models match FIFE observations.

Betts and Ball express some caution about the 1988 latent heat flux measurements. 1988 latent heat fluxes are just as strong as 1987 fluxes, even though 1988 was a drought. Also, FIFE (E-P) does not match measured soil moisture changes. There could be several reasons for these features in the FIFE data. Caution must be exercised in the comparisons.
