I. Introduction
  • A. Recent technological advances have permitted sophisticated meteorological models to be run on computer workstations rather than supercomputers.

  • B. Availability of high-volume data storage, high-speed retrieval, and global communication systems makes data more widely accessible for research and operational applications.

  • C. Potential exists for "model-enhancement" of agroclimatic databases

  • D. Opportunities exist for training scientists from the international community, particularly scientists from developing countries, in the use of these models for agroclimatic applications.
