PIRCS,

Things are looking very bad. We may have lost data on /usr/people/pircs which is not backed up. File systems /usr/people/$USERNAME (your home directories) and the ftp and WWW files have been totally recovered either from xfs_rescue or from tape (they were backed up to tape). However, since /usr/people/pircs was so large and people were using the 8mm to make tapes to, I did not have a back-up plan for this system. I of course have created one and have the tapes ready for it, but that does not help us in the current situation. I still have two actions to try.

1) move unit 1 of the pircs directory to another lun/slot and try to recover from there

2) try a low level drive access program for set-up and configuration of drives.

Normally this is used only at the factory (so the documentation tells me) so details on it's use are sketchy. I am in touch with SGI asking them for advise on it's use. Currently I am rebuilding another of the 9 gig drives into a new unit 1 and will try to add this to the pircs drive set. This will be a last ditch attempt to at least recover 2/3's of the files (unlikely to be that high, but a theoretical possibility). While that drive is being created I am researching the above two options so that I have all the details I need for when I am in front of the computer. It seems one of the 9 gig units had a hardware level failture. This failure affected 2 other drives on the machine, but I seem to have recovered fully those two units. Unfortunately the drive that failed was unit 1 of 3 (the first of the set) and as such contains the file structure information needed to recover the file system. I will keep you up to date on the actions I am taking and the progress, if any.
