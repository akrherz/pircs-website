1 - Introduction

 Full participation in PIRCS simulations requires you to register your model. Please fill out the registration form at the PIRCS ftp site and send it to us. Please contact us if any question arises.

The full two-month boundary and initial conditions for experiment 1a can also be picked up from the PIRCS ftp site. The full dataset is in the same format as the betatest except for a slight change in boundary condition (BC) files.

Experiment 1a covers the period from 0000 UTC May 15 to 0000 UTC July 15, 1988 and experiment 1b (to be ready soon) covers that from 0000 UTC June 1 to 0000 UTC July 31, 1993. The initial conditions for the atmosphere is at the commencement time while that for the sea-lake-surface temperature and soil moisture are the monthly averages. (See betatest for details.) As in the betatest, this full dataset consists of data themselves and sample reading routines. Please fill out the registration form (/PIRCS/FULL.RELEASE/registration_form) and send it to us. Please contact us if any question arises.

3 - Updated list of variables to be collected

 In the time since the original design of experiment 1, some changes have been made to the list of required model outputs. The major changes were the inclusion of low level jet (LLJ) diagnosis and surface hydrology. The following tables list the current output variables that must be saved to participate in the PIRCS intercomparison.

HOURLY

Average of data over the previous hour (e.g., output at 12 UTC is the average over the period 11 UTC - 12 UTC).

ID 1. Surface Fluxes Unit Frequency
a incident solar (shortwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
b reflected solar (shortwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
c upward terrestrial (longwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
d downward terrestrial (longwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
e sensible heat flux Watts / sq meter hourly
f deep-soil heat flux Watts / sq meter hourly
g latent heat flux (evapotranspiration) Watts / sq meter hourly
h precipitation mm /month hourly
ID 2. Top-of-atmosphere fluxes Unit Frequency
a net solar (shortwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
b upward terrestrial (longwave) radiation Watts / sq meter hourly
ID 3. Mass Unit Frequency
a surface pressure Pa hourly
b precipitable water m hourly
ID 4. Vertical profiles at selected grids* Unit Frequency
a temperature K hourly
c specific humidity kg/kg hourly
d zonal (east-west) wind m/s hourly
e meridional (north-south) wind m/s hourly
f vertical velocity m/s hourly
g geopotential height m hourly
h pressure Pa hourly
ID 5. Low-level Jet Level Frequency
a zonal wind (m/s) 500 m AGL hourly
b meridional wind (m/s) 500 m AGL hourly
ID 6. Mixed-Layer Depth Unit Frequency
a mixed-layer depth m AGL hourly

5-DAY AVERAGE

These output variables are average values for successive 5-day periods, yielding 12 periods for the 60-day simulation. Output should be interpolated to the levels 1000 mb, 925 mb, 850 mb, 700 mb, 500 mb, 400 mb, 300 mb and 200 mb. Horizontal wind components must be the true east-west and north-south components.

a temperature K 5-day
